Claude Week

For five days, the IDE stays closed. Everything flows through Claude Code CLI — real sprint work, real deliverables, just a completely different way of getting there. Think of it less as an experiment and more as a week where you get to find out what this thing can actually do.

It'll feel weird on Monday. By Friday you'll have opinions.

"If you're typing code, you're doing it wrong." — yes, really.
The week
Monday Stop commanding. Start planning.

Before writing a single line, have a conversation. Use Plan Mode to think through architecture with Claude — let it push back on your ideas, ask questions you hadn't thought of, propose approaches you wouldn't have. Then hand off the entire git workflow too: commits, PRs, all of it. Your hands are going to feel very idle. That's fine.

Tuesday What does Claude actually know?

The quality of Claude's output is directly proportional to the quality of context you give it. Today is about getting deliberate with that — trimming your CLAUDE.md to what actually matters, working from Figma screenshots as the source of truth, and learning to give Claude exactly the right information at exactly the right moment. You'll also run /insights today for the first time, which shows you your own usage patterns. Consider it your baseline.

Wednesday Close the loop.

Claude writes it, Playwright verifies it visually, PR feedback comes in, Claude addresses it. Today is about running a full cycle — from implementation to reviewed, merged PR — without touching the IDE once. Where does the loop hold? Where does it break down?

Thursday What keeps coming up?

By now you've probably repeated a few workflows enough times to notice them. Today is about going further: sub-agents handling parallel work, and — for those ready — building custom skills for the patterns worth automating. Ask Claude to explain how it did something, not just what it did.

Friday Put it all together.

Finish the sprint. Then run /insights one more time and actually compare it to Tuesday's baseline — you'll have real data on how your patterns shifted across the week. The afternoon ends with the showcase: a live demo of the best thing you built.

Friday showcase

Demo the most interesting thing you built this week.

Five minutes, live, with the whole team. Show what you made, show one moment where Claude genuinely surprised you, and tell us what you'd do differently. No slides required. No polish required. Just show the thing.

Anything goes.
